Communicating complexity to others, particularly the non-technical, is one of the biggest challenges facing developers. And it's something I've been considering a lot lately. Mainly because I don't seem to be able to do it.

The other day I tweeted the following, which highlights my frustration...

Development is teaching me that people who don't understand something often believe it is less complicated than it actually is. #dev #php

— Rob Waller (@RobDWaller) June 23, 2017

Now, of course, you could ask, what does it matter? It's not the job of the 'normals' to understand technical complexity, that is what you are paid for... And to some degree you are correct, but like all good answers to big questions, we must begin with a drink. Or several, in my case...

Back in March, or whenever it was, I was on a stag do -- 'batchelor party' if you're American. now, as is typical of these sorts of things you often gravitate to your own kind... After a few drinks on the second night I got talking to the future brother in law, and what do you know, he was a software developer. He did AI, so was much smarter than I. Anyway we got talking and after a few more drinks he said something rather profound to me...

"The sole purpose of a software developer is to manage complexity."

I hadn't managed to ever express this thought before, but it immediately struck me as one of those natural truism. Like...
