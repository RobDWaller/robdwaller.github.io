The Fakers App

The Fakers App, was launched in 2012 and was a very successful product. The premise of the app was to do follower analysis on Twitter and highlight how many followers were either fake or inactive. This was a significant issue at the time because many people were buying fake followers for their Twitter accounts. This meant fellow users and organisations couldn't be sure how popular a Twitter account really was.

The app was used by over 1.5 million people, and it's blocking tool which helped users remove fake followers from their accounts blocked 10s of millions of fake accounts. The Fakers App was also mentioned in the media across the world including Mashable and in an interview on CNN.

The App was closed down in early 2020 as it was felt the app's time had passed and it was becoming increasingly difficult to maintain. All of the code from the app was open sourced on GitHub so other developers might be able to repurpose some of the ideas contained within.

There was also nice feedback from users of the app once it was closed down, this was touching and highlighted its value to people and organisations.
